Visa Guide
All major Australian visa types that lead toward permanent residency and citizenship, with key requirements and facts.
💼
Skilled Independent (Subclass 189)
PermanentPoints basedNo sponsorship

Who: Skilled workers not sponsored by employer, family, or state. Must be on the MLTSSL occupation list.

Requirements: At least 65 points (typically 80 or more to receive invitation), skills assessment, under 45, competent English.

What you get: Permanent residency from day one. Live and work anywhere in Australia.

Fee from approximately AUD 4,640. Recent rounds require 80 to 95 or more points for most occupations.
🏙️
Skilled Nominated (Subclass 190)
PermanentState nominated+5 bonus points

Who: Skilled workers nominated by a state or territory government. Each state has its own occupation list.

Requirements: At least 65 points plus 5 nomination bonus. Must commit to living in the nominating state for 2 years.

What you get: Permanent residency. Check each state website for current occupation lists and requirements.

🌿
Skilled Work Regional (Subclass 491)
Temporary (5 years)Regional+15 bonus points

Who: Skilled workers who want to live and work in regional Australia.

What you get: 5 year temporary visa. After 3 years living and working regionally, apply for Subclass 191 permanent residency. South Australia, Tasmania, and regional Queensland have strong programs with less competition.

💑
Partner Visa (Subclass 820 and 801 or 309 and 100)
Temporary then PermanentMarried or de facto

Who: Married or de facto partners of Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible NZ citizens.

Requirements: Genuine and continuing relationship. De facto needs 12 months together or a registered relationship. Both partners must be 18 or older.

What you get: Temporary visa first, then permanent approximately 2 years after lodgement if relationship is still genuine.

Fee starts from AUD 9,365 for most applicants. One of the most expensive visas. Strong documentation of the relationship is essential.
🏗️
Employer Nominated Scheme (Subclass 186)
PermanentEmployer sponsored

Who: Skilled workers nominated by an approved Australian employer. Three streams: Direct Entry (3 years experience), Temporary Residence Transition (2 years on 482 with same employer), and Labour Agreement.

What you get: Permanent residency. Must generally stay with nominating employer for 2 years.

🎓
Student Visa (Subclass 500)
TemporaryStudy pathway

Who: International students enrolled full time at an Australian registered institution.

What you get: Work up to 48 hours per fortnight during term. Time in Australia counts toward the 4 year citizenship residency clock. Studying in a regional area adds 5 bonus skilled migration points.

🙏
Humanitarian and Refugee Visas (200 to 204)
PermanentHumanitarian

Subclass 200 (Refugee), 202 (Global Special Humanitarian, requires Australian proposer), 203 (Emergency Rescue), 204 (Woman at Risk). All provide permanent residency and a pathway to citizenship after meeting residency requirements.

Visa fees, occupation lists, and requirements change regularly. Always verify at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au or consult a registered MARA migration agent before making decisions.

The Single Most Important Rule

There are 5 Australian Values questions in the test. You must answer all 5 correctly, no exceptions. Even if you score 19 out of 20 overall but get one Values question wrong, you fail the entire test and must sit it again.

Focus the majority of your study time on Chapter 4 of the official booklet. Know the Australian values cold before you sit the test.
📖
What the Test Covers
  • Chapter 1: Australia and its people History, geography, population, symbols, and the flag
  • Chapter 2: Democratic beliefs, rights and liberties Voting, freedom of speech, equality, and the rule of law
  • Chapter 3: Government and the law How government works, the Constitution, the legal system
  • Chapter 4: Australian values (all 5 MUST be correct) What it means to be Australian, mutual respect, democracy, and equality
📅
Suggested Study Plan (2 to 4 Weeks)
  1. Days 1 to 2: Download and read "Australian Citizenship: Our Common Bond" in full. Free at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au in 40 or more languages.
  2. Days 3 to 7: Take the official practice test on the Home Affairs website. Note every question you got wrong and reread that section of the booklet.
  3. Days 8 to 10: Focus entirely on Chapter 4. Memorise the core Australian values and what they mean in practical terms.
  4. Days 11 onwards: Repeat the practice test daily until you consistently score 100%. Listen to the Our Common Bond podcast (5 free episodes).

Insider Tips and Shortcuts
Real pathways and strategies that many people do not know about. These could save you years.
⚠️Not guaranteed, here are the tips anyway. Every situation is unique. Always verify with the Department of Home Affairs or a registered MARA migration agent before acting on any tip.
💍
Married to an Australian? You Have a Dedicated Pathway
Partner Visa leads directly to permanent residency

If you are married to or in a de facto relationship with an Australian citizen or PR, the partner visa pathway can be faster and more accessible than skilled migration. Your temporary partner visa time counts toward the 4 year citizenship residency clock.

  • Onshore: Subclass 820 then 801. Offshore: Subclass 309 then 100
  • Permanent visa approximately 2 years after lodgement
  • Same-sex couples fully eligible
Not guaranteed, relationship must be proven genuine with strong evidence
🤲
Not Married? De Facto Counts the Same
12 months together or a registered relationship qualifies

Australia treats de facto relationships the same as marriages for partner visas. Genuine relationship for at least 12 months, or a registered relationship in your state, is enough without being married. Register at your state registry office to count as proof without the 12 month wait.

Not guaranteed, de facto relationships require substantial documentary evidence
🌾
Moving Regionally Opens Faster and Easier Pathways
491 to 191 pathway, state nomination with less competition

Regional areas actively recruit skilled workers. Benefits include 15 bonus points for regional nomination on a 491 visa, less competition than capital cities, and a direct pathway to permanent residency (191) after 3 years. South Australia (outside Adelaide), Tasmania, and regional Queensland all have strong programs.

Not guaranteed, occupation and points requirements still apply
👨‍👩‍👧
Parent Was Australian? You May Skip the 4 Year Wait
Citizenship by descent bypasses all residency requirements

If at least one of your parents was an Australian citizen at the time of your birth, even if you were born overseas, you may be eligible for citizenship by descent. No 4 year clock, no citizenship test needed. Apply through ImmiAccount or an Australian Embassy.

Not guaranteed, specific rules apply based on your birth year and parent's citizenship status at time of your birth
🎂
Age 60 or Over? No Citizenship Test Required
Automatic exemption from the 20 question exam

Applicants aged 60 or over at time of application are automatically exempt from the citizenship test. All other requirements still apply, but no studying or exam is needed. You may still attend a brief interview.

Not guaranteed, all other eligibility criteria including residency still apply
⬆️
One English Test Can Add 10 Points Instantly
The highest ROI points boost available

Going from Proficient English (IELTS 7.0) to Superior English (IELTS 8.0) adds 10 points instantly. This is often easier and faster than gaining 10 points through additional work experience, which takes years. Invest in English coaching before any other improvement strategy.

Not guaranteed, English test results vary and tests are valid for 3 years
📁
Start a Documents Folder Today, Not When You Are Ready to Apply
The number one cause of citizenship delays is missing documents

Create a folder labelled "AusPath Docs" and save every document immediately: bank statements, payslips, visa letters, every time you enter or leave Australia. Overseas police clearances alone take 4 to 12 weeks. If you wait until you are ready to apply, these delays will push back your entire timeline.

Practical tip only, does not guarantee faster processing times
📊

Most people never check and assume they do not qualify

Most people assume they do not qualify for skilled migration without ever running the numbers. Use the Points Calculator tab right now. If you score 65 or above, you can submit an Expression of Interest. If you score 80 or above, you are competitive for most occupations.
